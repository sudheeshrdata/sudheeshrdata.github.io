Analyzing Sentiments Regarding ChatGPT Using Novel BERT: A Machine Learning Approach

Published in MDPI

This study analyzed 21,515 tweets to understand public sentiment towards ChatGPT using the BERT model. The results showed mixed feelings, with many users expressing positive views about its usefulness, while others raised concerns, particularly regarding its suitability for students. Achieving an accuracy of 94.96%, the study provides valuable insights into user perceptions and highlights areas for improvement. Future work will focus on expanding the dataset and exploring ways to ensure responsible AI deployment. Additionally, the study’s findings indicate that while the majority expressed positive sentiments towards ChatGPT, a minority raised concerns regarding its educational suitability, which provides valuable feedback for future enhancements.

Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers and Deep Learning Model for Analyzing Smartphone-related Tweets

Published in PeerJ

This study presents a BERT-based model for classifying sentiments in smartphone-related tweets, achieving 99% accuracy with preprocessed data. It outperforms several other models, including GRU and LSTM. The findings indicate that preprocessing improves model's performance and reduces computation time. The model's insights can help businesses understand public sentiment and improve branding. The proposed methodology can be implemented on other social media related sentiment analysis tasks. A detailed comparison of the proposed approach with machine learning and deep learning models demonstrates its superior performance. Future work will explore additional preprocessing techniques to enhance results.

Air Quality Index Analysis

The analysis focuses on evaluating air quality in Delhi for January 2023, aiming to provide a clear numerical representation of pollution levels and their potential impact on public health. The analysis examines the relationships between various pollutants, including CO, NO, NO₂, SO₂, PM₂.₅, PM₁₀, NH₃, and O₃. By identifying critical pollutant interactions, this project enhances the understanding of air quality fluctuations.

Key Insights
Strong positive correlations between CO, NO, NO₂, SO₂, PM₂.₅, PM₁₀, and NH₃ suggest shared emission sources or trends. O₃’s inverse correlation with other pollutants highlights its unique role in air quality. Poorer air quality observed on Wednesdays and Thursdays, pointing to specific pollution patterns. The findings inform targeted measures for improving air quality and reducing health risks.
